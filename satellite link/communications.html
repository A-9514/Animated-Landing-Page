Key Constellations & Orbits_

Geostationary Orbit (GEO)

Located ~35,786 km above the Equator. Satellites appear stationary from the ground, ideal for broadcasting and weather monitoring over specific regions. Examples: Intelsat, Eutelsat, GOES (Weather/Comm).

Status: Stable, High Latency.

Low Earth Orbit (LEO)

Orbiting much closer (160-2,000 km). Requires large constellations for continuous coverage. Offers low latency, making them suitable for global internet access. Examples: Starlink, OneWeb, Iridium (Voice/Data).

Status: Dynamic Coverage, Low Latency.

Medium Earth Orbit (MEO)

Orbits between LEO and GEO (2,000-35,786 km). Balances coverage area and latency. Often used for navigation systems but also some communication networks. Example: O3b Networks.

Status: Balanced Performance.

Specific Applications

Includes direct broadcast satellites (DBS) for TV, mobile satellite services (MSS) for satellite phones, VSAT networks for business data, and military communication systems (Milstar, MUOS).

Status: Task-Specific Networks Active.
