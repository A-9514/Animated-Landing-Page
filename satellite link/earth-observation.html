// Earth Observation Sensors_ ACTIVE

Earth Observation (EO) satellites are humanity's eyes in the sky, continuously monitoring our planet's land, oceans, atmosphere, and ice caps. They provide critical data for weather forecasting, climate change studies, disaster management, agriculture, and resource monitoring.

Primary Sensor Categories_

Meteorological Satellites

Monitor weather patterns, cloud cover, temperature, and atmospheric conditions. Operate in both GEO (like GOES, Meteosat for continuous regional views) and Polar Orbits (like NOAA, MetOp for global coverage).

Data Stream: Weather imaging, atmospheric sounding.

Optical Imaging Satellites

Capture detailed images of the Earth's surface using visible and near-infrared light, similar to a digital camera. Resolution varies widely. Examples: Landsat series, Sentinel-2, Planet Labs constellation, WorldView.

Data Stream: High-resolution imagery, land cover mapping.

Radar Satellites (SAR)

Use radar pulses to image the surface, capable of seeing through clouds and darkness. Excellent for monitoring surface deformation, ice movement, and ship detection. Examples: Sentinel-1, RADARSAT, TerraSAR-X.

Data Stream: All-weather surface imaging, interferometry.

Environmental Monitoring

Carry specialized instruments to measure ocean color/temperature, atmospheric gases (CO2, methane), vegetation health, sea levels, and ice sheet thickness. Examples: Sentinel-3, ICESat-2, MODIS (on Terra/Aqua).

Data Stream: Climate variables, ecosystem health data.

Navigating the Globe_

Other constellations provide precise positioning data, guiding us anywhere on Earth.
