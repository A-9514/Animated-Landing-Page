// Scientific Research Satellites_

Beyond practical applications, satellites are invaluable tools for fundamental science. They provide unique vantage points to study Earth, our solar system, and the distant universe, free from atmospheric interference.

Key Research Domains_

Space Telescopes (Astronomy)

Observe the universe across the electromagnetic spectrum (visible, infrared, ultraviolet, X-ray, gamma-ray). Examples: Hubble Space Telescope (HST), James Webb Space Telescope (JWST), Chandra X-ray Observatory, Spitzer (retired).

Mission: Unveiling cosmic origins, exoplanets, galaxy evolution.

Solar & Heliospheric Physics

Study the Sun's activity, solar wind, and its influence on the space environment around Earth (space weather). Examples: Solar Dynamics Observatory (SDO), Parker Solar Probe, SOHO.

Mission: Understanding solar flares, CMEs, space weather prediction.

Earth Science Research

Dedicated missions focusing on specific Earth systems with high precision, beyond operational EO. Examples: GRACE-FO (gravity field), SWOT (surface water), CloudSat/CALIPSO (clouds/aerosols).

Mission: Detailed climate modeling, understanding Earth processes.

Planetary Science

Orbiters, landers, and rovers sent to explore other planets, moons, asteroids, and comets within our solar system. Examples: Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter (MRO), Cassini (retired), Juno (Jupiter).

Mission: Exploring solar system formation, searching for life.

Fundamental Physics

Using the space environment to test theories of gravity, relativity, and particle physics. Examples: LISA Pathfinder (gravitational waves tech demo), Gravity Probe B (tested general relativity).

Mission: Testing the boundaries of known physics.
