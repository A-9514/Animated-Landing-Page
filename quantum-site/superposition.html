Superposition: Being Everywhere at Once (Sort Of)

One of the most counter-intuitive quantum phenomena. Superposition means a quantum system (like a qubit) can exist in multiple states simultaneously until it's measured.

Think of it like a spinning coin. While it's spinning, it's neither heads nor tails – it's in a superposition of both possibilities. Only when it lands (is measured) does it settle into a definite state (heads or tails).

Visualizing the Possibilities

Before Measurement

The qubit exists as a combination of probabilities for being 0 and 1. It holds information about both states.

{ α|0⟩ + β|1⟩ }

Measurement Causes Collapse:

Observing the qubit forces it into a single, definite classical state (either 0 or 1).

Either |0⟩ OR |1⟩

The Power of Parallelism

Because a register of N qubits can exist in a superposition of all 2N possible classical states, a quantum computer can, in a sense, perform calculations on all these states simultaneously. This is the source of quantum computing's potential power for specific tasks.
