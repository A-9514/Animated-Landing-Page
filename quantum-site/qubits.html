The Quantum Bit (Qubit)

The fundamental unit of quantum information. Unlike a classical bit, which must be either 0 or 1, a qubit can be 0, 1, or a superposition of both states simultaneously.

Classical Bit

Represents either 0 OR 1.

Like a light switch: definitively ON or OFF.

Quantum Bit (Qubit)

Can be 0, 1, or both at the same time (Superposition).

Think of it more like a dimmer switch or a spinning coin - it has possibilities until measured.

Why Does This Matter?

This ability to hold multiple values allows quantum computers to explore a vast number of possibilities simultaneously. For certain problems, this leads to exponential speedups compared to classical algorithms.
